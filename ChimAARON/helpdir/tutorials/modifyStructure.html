Modifying Structures

The Modify Structure tool simplifies swapping ligands, adding or changing substituents, and making fused rings. To start the tool, go to Tools → ChimAARON → Modify Structure.

Making Fused Rings

Suppose you want to make tetralin and you have a benzene structure. If your don't have a benzene structure, you can open the benzene from the AaronTools ring library.

Begin by opening the Modify Structure tool. Next, select two atoms on the benzene that are ortho to each other:

In the Close Ring tab of the tool, press current selection. For the ring fragment, press from library.... We'll use cyclohexane. If you have cyclohexene in your library, it might produce a better result.

To make the fused ring, press close ring. You should now have a tetralin molecule.

Changing Substituents

For this tutorial, we'll walk you through making trinitrotoluene from benzene. If your don't have a benzene structure, you can open the benzene from the AaronTools ring library.

Begin by opening the Modify Structure tool. Next, select three hydrogen atoms that are all meta to eachother:

In the Substitute tab of the tool, press current selection. Press from library.... In the substituent list, find "NO2" and press OK.

Pressing substitute should give you trinitrobenzene. Next, select one of the remaining hydrogen atoms:

Back in the tool, press current selection. You can find the methyl substituent in the substituent list like before, or you can just type "Me" into the Substituent: box.
